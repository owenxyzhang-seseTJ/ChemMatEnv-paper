# Claim Evidence Ladder

Use this ladder to control claim strength.

| Rung | Claim Type | Minimum Support |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Observation | A measured result under defined conditions. |
| 2 | Comparative | Named comparator and matched conditions. |
| 3 | Structural | The claimed feature is verified by independent characterization. |
| 4 | Mechanistic | At least two independent evidence types, or one direct probe plus one exclusion argument. |
| 5 | Scope | Multiple material, substrate, or condition classes tested. |

## Promotion Rules

- Do not move from rung 2 to rung 4 by wording alone — the gap between "better than a baseline" and "this mechanism explains it" is not a language change, it is a new evidence requirement.
- A mechanism verb such as `drives`, `governs`, or `enables` needs rung 4 support, because these verbs assert causation and a single correlative result cannot carry it.
- A scope phrase such as `general`, `platform`, or `broadly applicable` needs rung 5 support, because scope claims are tested by breadth, not by strength in one system.
- If support is correlational, use `is associated with`, `is consistent with`, `suggests`, or `may reflect` — these preserve what the data actually shows and survive reviewer scrutiny.

## Revision Actions

- Downgrade unsupported claims.
- Add named baselines and conditions.
- Move broad implications to the final sentence only after evidence has earned them.
- Mark unsolved evidence gaps in the risk register.
