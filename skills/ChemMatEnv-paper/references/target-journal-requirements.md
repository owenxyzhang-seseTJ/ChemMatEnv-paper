# Target Journal Requirements Protocol

Use this protocol before drafting for a named journal. The requirements packet fields (journal, article type, guideline URL, title/abstract/main-text/display-item/reference limits, required sections, template, etc.) are listed in SKILL.md; this file covers source priority, search queries, and drafting rules — the parts SKILL.md does not.

## Source Priority

1. Official journal author guidelines.
2. Official publisher instructions for authors.
3. Official manuscript templates or article-type pages.
4. Recent accepted articles only as a style reference, not as a replacement for guidelines.

Why this order: guidelines are normative (they state the limits a desk-check enforces); templates are descriptive of format but silent on word limits; accepted articles only show what passed, not what is currently required. Guidelines also change, so a newer official page outranks an older cached one.

## Required Search

Search the web live. Use queries such as:

- `[journal name] author guidelines manuscript format`
- `[journal name] article type word limit abstract`
- `[journal name] manuscript template docx latex`
- `[journal name] graphical abstract TOC graphic requirements`
- `[journal name] reporting summary supplementary information`

## Drafting Rules

- If a requirement is not found, write `not found in checked sources`, not a guess.
- If requirements differ by article type, ask for or infer the article type and mark the inference.
- Treat official current guidance as higher priority than cached local rules.
- Carry the requirements packet into manuscript drafting and review.
- If live search cannot be performed and the user has not provided official guidelines, stop before journal-specific drafting.
- Do not rely on memory for current word limits, title limits, display-item limits, template availability, or reporting requirements.

Why the last two matter: journal limits and reporting requirements drift between years and between article types; a confident-but-wrong word limit surfaces as a desk-reject or a forced last-minute cut, which is costlier than a stop-and-ask.

## Journal Family Quick Reference

Before drafting for a named journal, check live requirements — this table is a starting point, not a substitute for current author guidelines.

### ACS (American Chemical Society)

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **JACS** | Communication (≤4 journal pages), Full Article (~8-10 pages) | Graphical TOC required; SI is primary experimental repository; title ≤15 words recommended; abstract unstructured ~150 words |
| **ACS Nano** | Article, Letter, Review | Graphical TOC required; max ~8 figures; SI for full experimental detail |
| **Nano Letters** | Letter (≤5 journal pages) | Concise format; graphical TOC; SI carries full methods |
| **Chemistry of Materials** | Article, Review, Perspective | Emphasis on materials design rationale; characterization rigor expected |
| **ACS Catalysis** | Article, Letter, Review | Kinetic data + mechanistic evidence expected; turnover numbers/frequencies |
| **Environmental Science & Technology (EST)** | Article, Viewpoint, Review | Environmental relevance must be explicit; field validation preferred; detection limits required |
| **ACS Energy Letters** | Letter (≤5 pages), Review, Perspective | Energy-focused; rapid publication; graphical TOC |
| **ACS Central Science** | Article, Outlook | Broad audience; significance beyond subfield required |
| **Chemical Reviews** | Review (invited) | Comprehensive; must include author contributions to the field stated |

**ACS common:** active voice preferred; Methods either in SI (communications) or in main text (full articles); graphical TOC mandatory for most titles; ACS citation style (superscript numbers); requires ORCID.

### RSC (Royal Society of Chemistry)

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Energy & Environmental Science (EES)** | Communication, Full Paper, Review | Short communication format for urgent findings; broader energy/environment impact required |
| **Chemical Science** | Edge Article, Article, Review | Diamond OA; general chemistry scope; significance across subfields |
| **Journal of Materials Chemistry A/B/C** | Paper, Communication, Review | A (energy/sustainability), B (biology/medicine), C (optical/magnetic/electronic); 3 sub-journals |
| **Materials Horizons** | Communication, Review | High novelty threshold; new conceptual advance required |
| **Chemical Society Reviews** | Review (invited) | Tutorial Reviews for broader audience; must be authoritative |
| **Green Chemistry** | Paper, Communication, Review | Green/sustainable chemistry focus; E-factor and green metrics encouraged |
| **Nanoscale** | Paper, Communication, Review | Nanomaterials focus; broad scope across nano-disciplines |

**RSC common:** RSC citation style [1]; allows moderate editorial language ("interestingly"); template available in LaTeX; gold/green OA models vary.

### Wiley

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Angewandte Chemie International Edition** | Communication, Research Article, Review | Communication ~5 pages; colon-separated titles common; strong novelty required; graphical TOC |
| **Advanced Materials** | Communication, Full Paper, Progress Report, Review | 10 figures typical; declarative titles; inside cover/back cover art common |
| **Advanced Energy Materials** | Communication, Full Paper, Review | Energy-focused; device performance metrics expected |
| **Advanced Functional Materials** | Communication, Full Paper, Review | Function-focused; application demonstration expected |
| **Small** | Communication, Full Paper, Review | Micro/nano materials; broad scope |
| **Advanced Science** | Research Article, Review | OA; cross-disciplinary appeal |

**Wiley common:** longer compound sentences tolerated; Wiley citation style [1]; figure-heavy layout; tolerates declarative/finding-led titles.

### Nature Family

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Article, Letter, Brief Communication | Summary paragraph required; Methods at end; ~50 ref cap; broad audience; no em dashes in prose |
| **Nature Materials / Chemistry / Energy / Nanotechnology** | Article, Letter, Review | Summary paragraph; ~30-50 refs; broad significance beyond subfield; Methods at end |
| **Nature Communications** | Article, Review | ~5,000 words incl. Methods; 10 display-item cap; 150-word abstract; ~60 refs; Data Availability statement required |
| **Communications Chemistry / Materials / Earth & Environment** | Article, Review | OA; Nature Communications-level rigor but narrower scope |
| **Nature Sustainability** | Article, Analysis, Review | Sustainability-focused; policy and societal relevance expected |

### Science Family

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Science** | Report (~2,500 words), Research Article (~4,500 words) | Summary required; Methods in SI; broad audience; ~30 refs for Reports |
| **Science Advances** | Research Article, Review | OA; ~5,000 words; Supplementary Materials flexible; broad scope |

### Cell Press

| Journal | Article Types | Key Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Chem** | Article, Review, Perspective | Chemistry full picture; strong mechanistic depth; broad chemical audience |
| **Joule** | Article, Report, Review | Energy performance metrics; device-level demonstration expected; techno-economic analysis valued |
| **Matter** | Article, Review, Perspective | Materials design focus; strong structure-property narrative; computational + experimental integration valued |

## Common Desk-Rejection Triggers by Family

- **ACS**: Graphical TOC missing or generic; insufficient characterization for claimed structure; scope too narrow for JACS/ACS Nano.
- **RSC**: Insufficient comparison to the state of the art; EES: no clear energy/environment advance demonstrated.
- **Wiley**: Overclaimed novelty; insufficient mechanistic depth for Angewandte; figure quality below production standard.
- **Nature family**: Significance too narrow for a broad audience; Methods insufficiently detailed; overclaim in summary paragraph.
- **Science family**: Same as Nature plus: Report format too short for the evidence chain; no field-level significance.
- **Cell Press**: Article reads like a specialist paper, not a broader conceptual advance; missing the "full picture" narrative arc.
